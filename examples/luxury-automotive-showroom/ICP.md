# Ideal Customer Profile — Luxury Digital Showroom

## Who we sell to

**Primary ICP: Independent luxury/exotic dealerships**
- Sells six-figure-plus vehicles (Ferrari, Lamborghini, McLaren, Bentley, Rolls-Royce, high-end pre-owned exotics)
- Independently owned, not a franchised mega-group with an in-house dev team
- 1-5 locations, $5M-$50M+ in annual inventory turnover
- Currently running a generic dealership-platform site (Dealer.com, DealerSocket, CDK) or a dated custom site (3+ years old)
- Decision-maker is the owner/GM, not a marketing department — fast sales cycle, single point of contact

**Secondary ICP: Luxury car brokers / private sales concierges**
- No physical showroom, sells by appointment/relationship
- Brand and discretion matter more than inventory volume
- Needs the site to function as a credibility/trust signal more than a transaction engine

**Tertiary ICP: Multi-location exotic dealership groups**
- 3+ locations under one brand
- Best fit for the productized template tier — same shell, swapped media/inventory per location
- Decision-maker is marketing director or ops lead, longer sales cycle, procurement involved

## Buying triggers (when they're in-market)
- Recently took on a new high-end brand line or franchise
- Noticeably behind competitors who've redone their site in the last 12 months
- Opening a new location or showroom
- Frustrated with lead quality from their current site (tire-kickers, no-shows)
- Rebranding or repositioning upmarket (e.g., moving from "used exotics" to "curated collection")

## Disqualifiers (not a fit)
- Mainstream/volume dealerships (sub-$60K average vehicle price) — different buyer psychology, ROI math doesn't hold
- Dealerships wanting a full e-commerce checkout flow (financing, online purchase) — different build entirely, not this product
- Anyone wanting the cheapest possible site — they'll churn at the first invoice; not worth the sales cycle

## Where to find them
- Instagram/social: exotic car accounts tagging their dealership, dealership-run car-spotting content
- Industry events: Cars & Coffee circuits, exotic car auctions (RM Sotheby's, Barrett-Jackson), SEMA
- Referral path: luxury car wrap/detailing shops, high-end auto photographers, exotic car rental/concierge services — they already touch this buyer
- Direct outreach: identify dealerships with outdated sites via a simple audit (page speed, mobile experience, stock template tells) and lead with the demo, not a pitch deck

## Messaging that resonates
- Lead with the demo, not the tech stack — "here's what your site could look like" beats "we use React Three Fiber and GSAP"
- Anchor on buyer psychology: "your buyer already trusts a private bank's website more than a used-car lot's — does yours feel like one?"
- Avoid generic "we do web design" positioning — position as a specialist in this single vertical, which justifies premium pricing and shortens the sales cycle

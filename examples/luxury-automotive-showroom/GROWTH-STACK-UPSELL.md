# The Growth Stack — Post-Launch Upsell Offer

For clients who've already shipped the digital showroom (CLIENT-PROPOSAL.md tier 1, 2, or 3). This is presented 2-3 weeks after launch, once they've seen real leads come through the form — not during the initial sales process.

---

## Why now (the pitch framing)

> "Your new showroom is generating leads that didn't exist before. Right now those leads sit in [email/CRM] until someone on your team gets to them. Every hour of delay on a six-figure buyer is an hour a competitor's site — or a competitor's salesperson — gets to them first. Here's how we close that gap, and then how we keep new buyers coming back to the site without you lifting a finger."

Lead with a number specific to them if you have it: "You've gotten {{lead_count}} private viewing requests since launch — how fast is your team actually responding to those?"

---

## Tier 1: AI Lead Response & Qualification

**What it does:** The moment someone submits the "Request Private Viewing" form, an AI agent:
- Sends an instant personalized reply (not a generic autoresponder — references the specific car/inventory they asked about)
- Asks 2-3 qualifying questions (budget range, financing vs. cash, timeline) via text/email conversation
- Books a call directly on the sales team's calendar, or flags hot leads for an instant Slack/SMS alert
- Logs everything to their CRM or a simple dashboard if they don't have one

**Why it's the easiest upsell:** It plugs directly into the form they already have. No new build, no new design — just intelligence layered on top of what they're already running.

**Investment:** $3,000–$6,000 one-time build + $300–$600/month for hosting, monitoring, and monthly tuning.

**Proof point to use:** "Dealerships that respond to a lead in under 5 minutes are dramatically more likely to convert it than ones that respond same-day — this makes that the default, automatically, even at 11pm on a Saturday."

---

## Tier 2: Content & Inventory Automation

**What it does:** An automation pipeline that:
- Generates a weekly social post (image + caption) for each new inventory arrival, in their brand voice
- Sends an automated email blast to their existing customer list when new inventory drops, segmented by the kind of car each past buyer showed interest in
- Refreshes the showroom site's inventory section automatically if they're on the CMS-backend tier

**Why it works as the second upsell:** Once lead response is automated, the bottleneck moves upstream — they need more qualified traffic hitting the site. This keeps the marketing engine running without hiring a social media manager.

**Investment:** $2,500–$5,000 one-time setup + $500–$1,000/month for ongoing generation and oversight.

**Proof point to use:** "Most dealerships post inventory once and let it sit. This keeps new arrivals in front of past buyers automatically — no extra headcount."

---

## Tier 3: Full Growth Retainer (Bundles Tier 1 + Tier 2)

**What it includes:**
- Everything in Tier 1 and Tier 2, managed as one relationship
- Monthly performance review call — lead volume, response times, conversion rates
- Quarterly site refresh (new media, seasonal messaging, inventory-driven landing page tweaks)
- Priority support — same-week turnaround on any request

**Investment:** $1,500–$2,500/month (replaces the standalone $500-1.5K/mo retainer in CLIENT-PROPOSAL.md once they're on the full stack — position as "we roll the build-phase retainer into this once you're ready to scale").

**Proof point to use:** Frame this as the difference between "a website" and "a growth system." The site got them in the door; this is what keeps them ahead of every other dealership in their market for the next two years, not just at launch.

---

## Sequencing rules

1. Never pitch Tier 1 before launch — it dilutes the core offer and creates decision fatigue. Wait until they've seen real lead volume.
2. Don't bundle Tier 1 + Tier 2 in the same pitch. Sell Tier 1 first, prove it works for 30 days, then introduce Tier 2 as "the natural next step now that leads are being handled."
3. The Tier 3 retainer pitch only happens after both Tier 1 and Tier 2 are live and the client has seen results from each — it's a consolidation offer, not a cold pitch.
4. If a client declines Tier 1, don't push Tier 2 — diagnose why first (budget, trust, or they don't see the lead-volume problem yet) before offering anything else.

---

## Objection handling specific to this upsell

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "We already have someone who responds to leads" | "This doesn't replace them — it catches the ones that come in outside business hours, or while your team's on the floor with a walk-in. It's a safety net that turns into your fastest responder." |
| "We don't want it to feel robotic" | Show the qualifying-question script — it's written in their voice, not a generic bot tone. Offer a one-week trial before committing. |
| "How is this different from what [CRM tool] already does?" | Most dealership CRMs notify a human — they don't carry on a qualifying conversation or respond in real time at 2am. This is the layer between "form submitted" and "human takes over." |
| "We want to see results from the site first" | Correct instinct — that's exactly why this is pitched at week 2-3, not week 1. Suggest revisiting in 2 weeks with their actual lead numbers. |
